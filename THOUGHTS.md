# Some thoughts of the founder about the future of humanity

With the arrival of Agentic AI it's pretty clear silicon brains are very efficient for complex reasoning and executing tasks, sooner or later they will be better than humans in pretty much every single job requiring reasoning and/or brute force if combined with robotics. The Liberal-Democratic Status Quo is going to change really fast in the next trhee decades, since selling goods and services is the very base of the free market capitalist system, and millions of jobs will soon be replaced with AI machines, the purchasing power of the many or medium income class is in real danger, sure, tons of oportunities will be created for us in the IT sector, but not for the most, something needs to be done to avoid the System to collapse under it's own weight. One way could be a Universal Basic Income, but that's not nearly enough, riots and civil wars are not usually caused because of hunger, but because extreme of inequallity in a society. We truly believe in the private sector and friendly competition contributing to efficiency, since State by itself can not control nor produce all the variety of goods and servicies the Market demands, it's not good at optimization nor innovation, but it's usually better at guaranteeing basic human rights like security, education, healtcare, basic utilities, without banning the private offer.

With this project we aim to give access to a computer to every single person around the world, basically becoming a "take for granted" at every single home and office around the world, like a basic utility included within your Internet bill thanks to the open source Codec Terminal which every manufacturer is welcome to produce and improve, altough clearly paying a dirt-cheap license to be able to connect to our Backend. We also want to show manufacturing can be done differently, more efficiently and even more profitable for the investors behind the product. There's a big social movement around "The Right to Fix", and makes sense from the environmental and consumer standpoint, but not so much for companies looking for profit, what if instead of producing easily obsolete products becoming trash in afew month, we offer them extremely durable, repairable, recyclable products they could even repair themselves, but in exchange, they'd have to pay a rent, so factories and investors can profit indefinitely on them without need for extracting more resources than really needed?... Zero Trash = More Efficiency = More Profit for your company.

We have not unlimited raw materials to produce unlimited products on Earth, we're not a gallactic civilization yet, but we have smart products designers at our factories willing to rethink them for the whole life cycle. At Coffee Pie® we commit to returning the 100% of raw materials to the production chains, we will not compete with manufacturers, we will not compete with datacenters, but complement them by providing circular economy services and connecting the offer with the demand in real time, in exchange, we will control the Ads System, what is and what is not allowed to be advertised in the platform. We are a private company looking for profit, indeed, that's not a secret, but our project prioritizes environment and humanity over profit, it is open source for generating the trust of the community, not to "sell" their private data, we are not databrokers, but we want anyone, inependently of their origin, knowledge or puchasing power, to have access to all the information availible in the network, both free, open source if the user can understand it, and/or propietary if the user can pay for it.

We think that in the future there will not be private nor public companies, but "mostly private" or "mostly public" companies. That should depend in the nature of the goods and servicies provided, if it's a basic primary product or services required for life dignity, it should be provided by "mostly public" companies controlled by states, if it's a sumptuous o luxury product or service it should be provided by "mostly private" companies. "The State whenever necessary, the Market whenever possible."

Avolink
Founder and CEO
